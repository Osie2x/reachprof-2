Biography

Dr. Jordan Ellis is an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Ontario Regional University, where they have been a faculty member since 2019. Before joining ORU, Dr. Ellis completed a postdoctoral fellowship at the Vector Institute for Artificial Intelligence and received their Ph.D. from the University of British Columbia in 2017. Dr. Ellis leads the Structured Learning and Inference Lab (SLIL), which develops probabilistic and graph-based methods for machine learning with applications in scientific discovery and decision-making under uncertainty.

Dr. Ellis has published in premier venues including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, and the Journal of Machine Learning Research. Their work has been supported by NSERC Discovery Grants and a Canada CIFAR AI Chair.

Research Interests

  • Probabilistic graphical models
  • Graph neural networks for structured prediction
  • Variational inference and approximate Bayesian methods
  • Reinforcement learning from human feedback
  • Applications in drug discovery and molecular property prediction
